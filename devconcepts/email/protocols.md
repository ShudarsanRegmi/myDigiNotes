# 📧 Email Protocols

Email protocols are rules that define how emails are sent and accessed between clients and mail servers.

---

## 1. SMTP (Simple Mail Transfer Protocol)

SMTP is the standard protocol used for **sending emails** from the sender’s email client to the sender’s mail server, and for transferring emails between mail servers until they reach the recipient’s server.

### Key Features:

* Handles **outgoing mail only**
* Works as a **push protocol** (sends messages to destination servers)
* Queues emails and **retries delivery** if the receiving server is unavailable
* Common ports:

  * **25**
  * **587**
  * **465 (secure SMTP)**

---

## 2. POP3 (Post Office Protocol Version 3)

POP3 is used to **retrieve emails** by downloading them from the mail server to the user’s device.

### Key Features:

* Downloads emails for **offline access**
* Often **removes emails from the server** after download (configurable)
* Best suited for **single-device usage**
* Common ports:

  * **110**
  * **995 (secure POP3)**

---

## 3. IMAP (Internet Message Access Protocol)

IMAP allows users to **access and manage emails directly on the server**, keeping everything synchronized across devices.

### Key Features:

* Emails are **stored on the server**
* Synchronizes actions like:

  * Read
  * Delete
  * Folder organization
* Ideal for **multi-device access** (phone, laptop, web)
* Common ports:

  * **143**
  * **993 (secure IMAP)**

---

# ✅ Advantages of Email

* **Fast communication**: Messages delivered within seconds worldwide
* **Low cost**: No printing or postage required
* **Supports attachments**: Send documents, images, audio, etc.
* **Accessible anywhere**: Works on phones, laptops, and web
* **Easy record keeping**: Emails can be stored and searched
* **Group communication**: Use CC/BCC to reach multiple recipients
* **Improves productivity**: Useful for formal communication and coordination

---

# ⚠️ Limitations of Email

* **Spam and junk mail**: Unwanted promotional or malicious emails
* **Phishing risk**: Fake emails can steal sensitive information
* **Malware threats**: Attachments or links may contain viruses
* **Delivery delays**: Possible due to server or network issues
* **Size limits**: Restrictions on attachment sizes
* **Privacy concerns**: Emails can be intercepted if not secure
* **Miscommunication**: Tone can be misunderstood in text
