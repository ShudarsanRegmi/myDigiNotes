## :sd Concord Practice (high-trap questions)

These are **deliberately deceptive**. Focus on **subject identification**, not nearby nouns.

---

## 🔹 Section 1: Fill in the blanks

1. A number of students ______ (is/are) absent today.
2. The number of students ______ (is/are) increasing.
3. Each of the players ______ (is/are) responsible.
4. Either Ram or his friends ______ (is/are) coming.
5. Neither the teacher nor the students ______ (was/were) ready.
6. The jury ______ (has/have) given its verdict.
7. Mathematics ______ (is/are) my favorite subject.
8. The news ______ (is/are) shocking.
9. A bouquet of flowers ______ (smell/smells) nice.
10. Ten kilometers ______ (is/are) a long distance.

---

## 🔹 Section 2: Error spotting

11. One of the boys have done it.
12. Each of the students are present.
13. Bread and butter are my breakfast.
14. The quality of the apples are good.
15. Neither of the answers are correct.
16. Everyone have submitted their assignment.
17. The police is investigating the case.
18. A lot of water are wasted.
19. The committee have decided to postpone the meeting.
20. Measles are a dangerous disease.

## 🔹 Section 3: Choose the correct option

21. Either the manager or the employees ______ responsible.
    (a) is
    (b) are

22. Neither of the boys ______ completed the work.
    (a) has
    (b) have

23. A pair of shoes ______ missing.
    (a) is
    (b) are

24. There ______ many reasons for this.
    (a) is
    (b) are

25. Not only the students but also the teacher ______ present.
    (a) was
    (b) were

## 🔹 Section 4: Edge-case traps

26. One of the students who ______ absent was punished.
    (a) was
    (b) were

27. The only one of the players who ______ selected is injured.
    (a) was
    (b) were

28. More than one student ______ failed the exam.
    (a) has
    (b) have

29. A number of issues ______ raised.
    (a) was
    (b) were

30. The majority of the work ______ completed.
    (a) is
    (b) are

# ✅ Answer Key (with quick reasoning)

### Section 1

1. are → “a number of” = plural
2. is → “the number” = singular
3. is → “each” = singular
4. are → nearest subject “friends” (proximity rule)
5. were → nearest “students”
6. has → jury as one unit
7. is → subject name → singular
8. is → “news” always singular
9. smells → main subject = bouquet
10. is → distance = single unit

---

### Section 2

11. have → ❌ → **has** (one = singular)
12. are → ❌ → **is** (each = singular)
13. are → ❌ → **is** (bread and butter = one idea)
14. are → ❌ → **is** (quality = subject)
15. are → ❌ → **is** (neither = singular)
16. have → ❌ → **has** (everyone = singular)
17. is → ❌ → **are** (police = plural)
18. are → ❌ → **is** (water = uncountable singular)
19. have → ❌ → **has** (committee as one unit)
20. are → ❌ → **is** (disease name = singular)

---

### Section 3

21. are → closer subject “employees”
22. has → neither = singular
23. is → pair = singular
24. are → “many reasons”
25. was → closer subject “teacher”

---

### Section 4

26. were → refers to “students” (plural clause)
27. was → refers to “one” (singular focus)
28. has → idiomatic rule (important trap)
29. were → “a number of” = plural
30. is → “work” = uncountable

## 🧠 High-impact vocabulary (learn from this)

* **Proximity rule** → verb agrees with nearest subject
* **Collective noun** → group acting as one (committee, jury)
* **Notional agreement** → meaning-based agreement (police = plural)
* **Appositive distraction** → extra phrase confusing subject (e.g., “of flowers”)
